What is FreshSense?

FreshSense is a simple, easy-to-use system that “smells” the air inside mushroom farms. Imagine a small device, about the size of your hand, quietly working in the corner of a grow room. It reads the air every second, looking for unusual odors that may signal problems such as mold, bad bacteria, or issues with the growing material.

Once FreshSense detects something unusual, it sends a quick alert to the farmer’s phone. This means the farmer can take action right away—long before the problem becomes visible.

FreshSense acts like a “nose that never sleeps,” helping farmers protect their harvest 24 hours a day.

Why Smell Matters

Most mushroom problems start quietly. Before mold becomes green, or before bacteria spreads, they release tiny smells that humans cannot notice. These smells show up hours or even days before any visible signs appear.

Farmers usually discover problems when it’s too late—when mushrooms look sick, when the trays turn strange colors, or when the room begins to smell odd. At this point, the contamination has already spread

FreshSense solves this by detecting early smells that are invisible and impossible to notice without technology. It gives farmers a “head start,” allowing them to stop problems early and save most—or even all—of the harvest.

How FreshSense Works

A Smart Odor Sensor

This is the small device placed in mushroom growing rooms. It checks the air for certain smells that are known to be early signs of trouble. It doesn’t need water, sunlight, or complicated setup—just place it and turn it on

A Mobile App for Farmers

The app shows what FreshSense is detecting. If the air is clean and healthy, everything will appear green. If something unusual starts to happen, the app changes color and sends a message like:

• “Warning: Possible mold activity in Room 2.”

• “Alert: Air quality changing—check humidity or ventilation.

Farmers can then act quickly by adjusting temperature, cleaning a tray, improving airflow, or removing infected spots.

Everything is designed to be easy to understand so any farmer, no matter their background, can use it comfortably.
